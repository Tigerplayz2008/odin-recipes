Joseph's Ham & Cheese Melt Recipe

Description:

The Ham and Cheese Melt is a scrumptious and comforting sandwich that combines the rich flavors of ham and melted cheese. This delicious treat features layers of thinly sliced ham, a blend of gooey cheeses, and toasted bread. The combination of savory ham, creamy cheese, and perfectly melted goodness makes this melt a delightful choice for a quick and satisfying meal.

Preparation time: 10 minutes

Cooking time: 10 minutes

Yield: 2 servings

Ingredints:

  • 4 slices of bread (your choice of bread, such as white, whole wheat, or sourdough)
  • 8 ounces (225 grams) thinly sliced ham
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (a combination of cheddar and Swiss works well)
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon butter, softened
  • Optional toppings: sliced tomatoes, onions, or pickles

Steps:

  1. Preheat a pan or griddle over medium heat.
  2. Lay out the slices of bread on a clean work surface. Spread mayonnaise evenly on one side of each slice.
  3. Divide the ham into 2 equal portions. Place one portion of ham on the mayonnaise side of 2 bread slices.
  4. Sprinkle half of the shredded cheese evenly over the ham on each slice.
  5. If desired, add any additional toppings, such as sliced tomatoes, onions, or pickles.
  6. Place the remaining bread slices, mayonnaise side down, on top of the ham and cheese to form sandwiches.
  7. Spread the softened butter on the outsides of the sandwiches.
  8. Place the sandwiches in the preheated pan or griddle. Cook for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until the bread turns golden brown and crispy, and the cheese starts to melt.
  9. Carefully flip the sandwiches using a spatula and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes until the cheese is fully melted and the bread is toasted to your liking.
  10. Remove the sandwiches from the heat and let them cool for a minute or two. Cut each sandwich in half diagonally, if desired, and serve hot.
  11. Enjoy your mouthwatering Ham and Cheese Melt with your favorite side dish, such as potato chips, fries, or a fresh green salad.
